# digital-edition

A prototype developed during the course **"Coding the Digital Edition"**, part of the MA in Digital Humanities at the University of Göttingen, taught by Msc. Johanna Störiko.

## About this project

Despite the repository name, this is **not a digital edition** in the scholarly sense. It is closer to a **digital facsimile** — a browser-based presentation of *Mode und Heim*, Issue 25/1904, a German fashion and home magazine from the early twentieth century.

A proper digital edition would require a critical apparatus, scholarly research, and rigorous editorial annotation. This prototype does not claim to be any of that. It is an exercise — a way to get hands-on with the tools and decisions that go into making historical documents accessible online.

The prototype includes:

- **Facsimile layout** — the magazine pages presented in a structured browser interface
- **Image annotation** — the ability to annotate regions directly on the facsimile images
- **JSON import/export** — annotations can be exported as JSON and reloaded, making them portable and reusable

## Context

The course "Coding the Digital Edition" introduced the theory and practice of digital scholarly editing: how to encode texts with structured markup ([TEI/XML](https://tei-c.org/)), how to think about editorial decisions, and how to publish historical sources in a way that is both human-readable and machine-processable. This prototype reflects what I learned during those classes.
